Dr. Ahmed H. Zewail

I am a frank man... I have no political ambition, as I have stressed repeatedly that I only want to serve Egypt in the field of science and die as a scientist

A brief introduction

Ahmed Hassan Zewail was an Egyptian American chemist, known as the "Father of Femtochemistry". He was awarded the 1999 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his work on femtochemistry and became the first Egyptian to win a Nobel Prize in a scientific field... continue reading

Early Life and Education

Born on February 26, 1946, in Damanhur, Egypt, he received his primary and secondary education in public schools. He then wen on to receive his BSc and MSc degrees in Chemistry from Alexandria University.

Later in the 1960s, he travelled to the United States to conduct his PhD research, and received his PhD from The University of Pennsylvania.

Career

He received a postdoctoral position at the University of California, Berkeley, under the supervision of Charles Bonner Harris.

Following the aforementioned nomination, he was awarded a faculty appointment at the California Institute of Technology (CalTech), where he made the firs Linus Pauling Chair in Chemical Physics.

Dr. Zewail conceptualized, developed and pioneered the femtochemistry. He was known as the "Father of Femtochemistry", and was awarded the 1999 Noble Prize for Chemistry for his ground-breaking contributions in the chemical sciences domain.

Politics and Public Contributions

Dr. Zewail was nominated and participated in President Obama's Presidential Council of Advisors on Science and Technology (PCAST). In 2009, he became the first, and one of three, Science Envoies from the US to the muslim world.

Dr. Zewail had a firm belief that Egypt and the Arab world would not make it into the 21st centruy without a strong scientific foundation. He establsihed the "Zewail City of Science and Technology" in 2000, a dream that had been revived, manifested and became a reality after Egyptian revolution 2011.

Honours and Awards

For his significant contributions in the domain of Science, Dr. Zewail received several Honorary Doctorates from prestigius institutions: University of Oxford (1991), Katholieke Universiteit Leuven (1997), University of Lausanne (1997), Sapienza University of Rome (2000), and University of Cambridge (2003) to name a few.

In October 2006, Dr. Zewail received the Albert Einstein World Award of Science for "his pioneering development of the new field of femtoscience and for his seminal contributions to the revolutionary discipline of physical biology, creating new ways for better understanding the functional behavior of biological systems by directly visualizing them in the four dimensions of space and time.". He also received other international awards including the King Faisal International Prize (1989), the Othmer Gold Medal (2009), the Priestley Medal (2011) from the American Chemical Society, amongst international awards

In 1999, Dr. Zewail received Egypt's highest state honor, the Grand Collar of the Nile, awarded for his exceptional services to the nation.
